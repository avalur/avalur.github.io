AI in Robotics


Eureka: Human-Level Reward Design via Coding LLMs (ICLR 2024)
Further after the first project I would like to share a groundbreaking paper that was recently presented by Nvidia at top-level conference ICLR 2024. It is titled 'Eureka: Human-Level Reward Design via Coding Large Language Models.' In my opinion, the main feature of this paper is that it uses Large Language Models (LLMs) to design human-level reward functions. As you see, the task is really hard ‚Äî a robot hand should be able to balance and rotate the pen. It is a challenging task even for humans, and probably it is even more challenging for humans to explain how our muscles work in this case. But Coding LLMs can help us solve the problem of designing reward function for this hard task. And eventually the robot hand is able to balance and rotate the pen as some humans do!

2. Animations in Manim for Solving Geometry Problems

  • Develop specialized geometric tools to enhance Manim's capabilities for geometry problems
  • Create AI system to automatically generate Manim code from problem descriptions
  • Build converter to transform GeoGebra constructions into Manim animations



CSAI NUP. Fedor Baharev. Spring 2025.

Let's move on to the second project, which combines mathematical visualization with AI in a really creative way. Here students are working with Manim, the same animation engine used by the popular maths YouTube channel 3Blue1Brown. As you can see from this visualization, Manim is capable of creating really nice mathematical animations. This project has three ambitious goals: First, students are extending Manim's toolkit by developing specialized geometric tools. The second is building an intelligent system that can take a written geometry problem and automatically generate the Manim code needed to animate it. And third, students will build a bridge between GeoGebra and Manim by developing a converter. This will allow us to take existing geometric constructions from GeoGebra and transform them into dynamic Manim animations. The teamleader here is Fedor Baharev, one of our professors.

4. LLM for Generating Astronomy Olympiad Problems




  • Current Challenge: Existing LLMs fall short in generating high-quality olympiad problems that match Iternational Olympiad standards
  • Research Objectives: Develop specialized datasets and explore novel approaches for training AI models to generate problems






CSAI NUP. Alex Avdiushenko. Spring 2025.

The next project addresses a challenge in educational AI: using LLMs to generate high-quality astronomy olympiad problems. Let me highlight the two main aspects of this project:
First, we're addressing a crucial limitation in current technology. Despite their sophistication, existing LLMs struggle to generate astronomy problems that truly meet International Olympiad standards. These problems require not just factual knowledge, but deep understanding of astronomical concepts, mathematical reasoning, and the ability to create engaging, challenging scenarios.
Second, we're focusing on two key research goals:
‚Äî Developing specialized, carefully gathered datasets that capture the essence of high-quality olympiad problems
‚Äî Exploring novel training approaches to help AI models understand and replicate the complex reasoning required for these problems
