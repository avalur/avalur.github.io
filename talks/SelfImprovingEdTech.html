Now let me share my own workflow for teaching and lecture preparation.
FIRSTLY, I usually work in PyCharm complemented with the TeXiFy IDEA plugin. This combination brings together LaTeX, Python, and JavaScript all in one place. It allows using powerful autocompletion and inspections of PyCharm, and inverse search from pdf.
NEXT, let's discuss the AI Assistant. It is capable of assisting with translations in a broader sense. AI helper is not just restricted to translating languages; it can convert formats, elaborate short ideas into detailed descriptions, and even to oral speech, facilitate auto tests creation, and more.
THIRDLY, I'm using the GitHub Copilot plugin. It offers practical pieces of code, draft solutions, and generally helps speed up the writing of text content for my lectures and other materials.
LAST but not least, the Grazie plugin checks for typos and polishes long sentences. Its purpose is to ensure your text is clean, concise, and clear before reaching your audience.
Here I'd like to show the concrete example from my own experience. It is producing the problem for the International Mathematical Competition.
First of all, I have chosen an appropriate problem from ancient times of my teaching at Novosibirsk State University. There I organized math contests with tricky olimpiad tasks. So I need to translate the problem from Russian to English and to make a Latex format from Microsoft Word.
You see, with the help of AI assistant; I can do it in a few seconds. Here I simply copy-paste the answer to the working area. Then I check the result and look at the Grazie suggestions. Here I don't accept it, because it suggests something strange about additional dot in the text after the word "elements", but often it helps to polish the text.
Then I compile Tex sources and look at PDF file.
Then I need to write a solution, so I think through the solution and type it in Latex format. So firstly, I write x^2 = 0, then latex Rightarrow command goes. Here you can see that GitHub Copilot helps me to write the solution very quickly; it even makes simple calculation with math formulas.
Then I write the second part of the solution. Here I need to prove that the product being equal to zero is commutative. So that ab=0 if and only if ba=0. You see, sometimes I disagree with the Copilot's suggestions, but often it helps. Eventually, I check everything, recompile Tex sources and get the final PDF file done. Really fast, effective and convenient workflow, at least for me. Maybe you will consider using something similar for your own practice.

Parts of the future system

  • AI-Assisted Content Creation
    • Use AI to generate, refine, and improve educational content continuously
    • ChatGPT-style assistants that provide explanations or generate new examples for hard-to-understand topics
  • Multimodal Learning
    • Combine text, video, interactive simulations, and virtual/augmented reality for a rich, adaptable experience
    • Example: A physics concept could be taught via text, a video demo, or an interactive AR experiment, depending on the student's learning style
  • Gamification and Engagement
    • Include features like badges, levels, or challenges that evolve as students progress
    • Example: A student earns a badge for mastering a topic, unlocking new challenges tailored to their level
Now let’s talk about how technology plays a key role in creating self-improving educational content. First, we have AI-powered tools that continuously generate, refine, and improve educational content. These tools can act like ChatGPT-style assistants, capable of providing clear explanations or generating new examples to simplify complex or challenging topics for students. This ensures that the content stays fresh, adaptive, and accessible for every learner. Next, multimodal learning focuses on delivering education through various channels like text, videos, interactive simulations, or even virtual and augmented reality. For instance, imagine teaching a physics concept — some students might prefer reading about it, others might benefit from a video demonstration, and for some, an interactive AR experiment could make the concept click. The idea is to adapt to different learning styles for maximum impact. Lastly, we bring in gamification. Features like levels, and challenges will keep students engaged and motivated. For example, when a student masters a topic, they might earn a badge, which then unlocks new, more advanced challenges tailored to their progress. It turns learning into a rewarding and fun experience. These three elements—AI-driven content, multimodal learning, and gamification — are the building blocks for creating an adaptive, engaging, and future-ready educational system.
